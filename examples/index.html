Maryland All Sky Camera Network

Welcome

Welcome to the Maryland All Sky Network website. The goal of the network is to collect nightly images of meteors. With this data, we hope we will be able to better locate meteorites. We hope to eventually expand this network to more cameras and be able to process more data. This is intended to be the place where one can find the results of the processed data gathered by the network.

This project has been worked on by students in the Science, Discovery and the Universe Scholars Program at the University of Maryland-College Park, as well as high school students from Haiti. Both Elizabeth Warner and Dr. Peter Teuben served as mentors for this project. The students worked on implementing the Astride program to work with our images, automating the program, making the program more user friendly, and building this website to display our findings to the public.

Technical Details

Currently the Maryland All Sky Network is composed of 1 Starlight Xpress Oculus Camera. The camera runs from sunset to sunrise, taking thousands of photos. The photos have exposure times between 25 and 55 seconds depending on the conditions that night. These photos are then run through a program called Astride, which is a streak-detection software developed by Dae-Won Kim. Above, you can see an example of a photo of a meteor and how astride processes the image to detect where the streak is. In addition, we are experimenting with difference imaging as another form of streak detection.
